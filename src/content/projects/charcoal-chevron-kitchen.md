A whole-room fit-out for a Victorian rear extension. The cabinetry is
hand-painted in a quiet charcoal that holds its own against the light
flooding in from the garden.

The chevron oak floor was specified to run unbroken from the front of
the house all the way out through the kitchen — the new joinery sits on
it as if it had always been there.

A thin brass plinth picks up the brass cup pulls on every door and
drawer; small details that lift an otherwise restrained palette.
