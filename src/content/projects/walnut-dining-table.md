This dining table started as a single slab of black walnut, air-dried for
three years before it ever reached the workshop. I kept the natural live
edge on both long sides and filled the small voids with a dark resin so the
surface stays smooth for everyday use.

The base is hand-welded steel, blackened and waxed, designed to stay out of
the way and let the wood do the talking. The whole piece is finished with
several coats of hardwax oil — a finish that can be repaired at home rather
than needing a full refinish.

Built to comfortably seat eight, with room to spare.
