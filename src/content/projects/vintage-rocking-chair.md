This rocking chair came in with a broken cane seat, loose joints, and a
finish worn down to bare wood in places. The ash frame was sound, so the
work was about respect rather than reinvention.

Every joint was taken apart, cleaned, and re-glued. The seat was hand-caned
in the traditional pattern to match the original. The frame got several
thin coats of shellac — the same finish it likely wore when it was new —
rubbed back between coats.

The goal with any restoration is that the piece looks cared for, not new.
